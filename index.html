Our goal with Pocket Racer is to enable researchers to tackle difficult problems concerning high-speed autonomy through scalable methods. To achieve that, we've made our miniature platforms open-source as to encourage autonomous races to race together and have fun!

Figure 1: Pocket Racer chassis

Pocket Racer x IEEE

In an effort to realize our goal of making autonomous racing fun, we've connected with the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers at the University of California, Los Angeles to allow undergraduate students to race their autonomous vehicles against each other. Check out what the Pocket Racer team has to say:

"Pocket Racer is a mini F1 sandbox for roboticists. It’s racing, with code.”
Jesse Cha – UCLA PhD Candidate / Pocket Racer Research Lead

"The Pocket Racer curriculum is full of surprises. You not only have the opportunity to engineer your own customizable RC car, but enjoy the experience of racing your design against others. It's really self-rewarding on its own."
Jeremy Louie – Curriculum Developer / Pocket Racer Undergraduate Researcher

"Pocket Racer provides an opportunity for interested students of all disciplines to gain experience in both hardware and software engineering."
Aaron Kuo – Curriculum Developer / IEEE Aircopter Lead
